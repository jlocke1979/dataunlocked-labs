Life Flow — Sources and Limitations
====================================


Primary data sources
--------------------

Organ Procurement and Transplantation Network (OPTN) / United Network for Organ
Sharing (UNOS)
  - OPTN National Data Reports (1988–2025 reporting period; 2025 figures where
    cited in the show)
  - OPTN Advanced Data Reports (donor recovery DSA to transplant center flows)
  - Public portals: HRSA OPTN data reports and UNOS advanced reporting tools

Scientific Registry for Transplant Recipients (SRTR)
  - SRTR Annual Data Report (context and reference framing)
  - SRTR transplant patient journey diagram (2023; supplemental reference)
  - SRTR transplant system reference diagram (supplemental reference)

Organ Donation Alliance
  - People-Driven Transplant System Map (SRTR Task 5; supplemental reference
    diagram)

UNOS
  - Public data resources and transplant system diagram (2023; supplemental
    reference)

Illustrative / prototype content in the show (e.g., patient survival prototype)
is labeled in-slide as pending full OPTN/SRTR validation.


Audit notes and limitations
-------------------------

- Data were sourced primarily from OPTN, SRTR, UNOS, and related public transplant
  datasets. Visualizations were created for educational and analytical purposes.

- Several visualizations use aggregated, normalized, or transformed data to improve
  readability and support storytelling.

- Geographic visualizations use publicly available transplant center and DSA
  information. Some node coordinates were approximated or manually adjusted where
  exact locations were unavailable or inconsistent.

- Network arc visualizations illustrate relationships and movement patterns within
  the transplant system. They should not be interpreted as exact transportation
  routes.

- Multi-organ transplant views were simplified and/or normalized to support
  comparison across categories.

- Assignment folders under apps/life_flow/assignments/ are retained for
  reproducibility; the final show embeds or reuses code and data from those
  iterations.

- All analyses and visualizations were manually reviewed. Additional validation
  is recommended before operational or policy use.


Use of generative AI
--------------------

ChatGPT and Cursor AI were used for brainstorming, debugging, code review,
editing, navigation design, and presentation refinement.

All analytical decisions, data sourcing, interpretation, visual design choices,
and final presentation content were reviewed and approved by the author.
