About the Uplands

The Uplands is a neighborhood founded in 1902 in the heart of Peoria, Illinois. We take pride in our historic homes, tree-lined boulevards, diversity of residents, and close-knit community.

Neighborhood Overview

Within easy walking distance of our homes are a supermarket, a pharmacy, an Asian market, and several unique restaurants. Residents also enjoy walking to events at Bradley University and playing in Bradley Park.

The Uplands Residential Association (URA) is open to all neighborhood residents and is a very strong and active organization. Annual membership dues are $20 and support neighborhood programs, communications, and shared stewardship.

Historic Character

Founded at the turn of the twentieth century, the Uplands is known for distinctive architecture, mature street trees, and carefully maintained boulevards. The neighborhood's physical character reflects more than a century of residential life in central Illinois.

Detailed history content and archival photographs will be added in a future update.

Association Governance

The Uplands Residential Association meets on the second Thursday of the month at 7:00 pm in the basement of Bradley Epworth Church, at the corner of Columbia Terrace and Elmwood Avenue. There is no regular meeting in December, September, or July.
