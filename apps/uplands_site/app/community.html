Community

The Uplands thrives on neighbor participation — from monthly coffees and the annual picnic to committees that welcome newcomers and keep each block connected.

Welcoming Committee

The Welcoming Committee helps new residents feel at home in the Uplands. Volunteers provide neighborhood orientation packets, introduce newcomers to association meetings and events, and answer common questions about parking, garbage collection, and local resources.

  • Welcome packets for new homeowners and renters
  • Neighborhood map with key contacts and amenities
  • Introduction to URA meetings, committees, and volunteer opportunities

Committee chair contact information and a volunteer sign-up form will be added here.

Block Captains

Block captains serve as liaisons between the URA board and residents on their street or block. They share newsletter reminders, relay neighborhood concerns, and help coordinate block-level communication during events like the annual garage sale or snow removal updates.

  • One captain per block or street segment, as needed
  • Distributes meeting notices and urgent neighborhood updates
  • Collects feedback for board and committee consideration

A directory of current block captains by street will be published here.

Neighborhood Activities

Monthly coffees are held in neighborhood homes, with hosts providing coffee and refreshments. The annual picnic takes place in September, the Saturday after Labor Day — grilled food is provided; neighbors bring a dish to share.
