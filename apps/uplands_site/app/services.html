Services & Programs

Neighborhood-funded programs and volunteer efforts that keep the Uplands safe, beautiful, and connected throughout the year.

Boulevard Gardening

Volunteers create and maintain plantings along the Columbia Terrace and Institute boulevards. Work includes seasonal planting, weeding, mulching, and coordinating native and ornamental species that complement the neighborhood's historic streetscape.

  • Spring and fall work days open to all residents
  • Native plant restoration along shared boulevard spaces
  • Coordination with the Neighborhood Beautification committee

Volunteer sign-up details and project photos will be added here.

Snow Removal

The Uplands Snow Removal Fund supports neighborhood-wide clearing of public sidewalks after significant snowfall. When accumulation reaches two inches or more, a contracted service clears walks along Elmwood, Institute, Glenwood, Maplewood, Parkside, and selected segments of Main Street and University.

  • Clearing occurs after city street plows finish in the Uplands
  • Private driveways are not cleared, except where they meet public sidewalks
  • Residents may contribute to the fund at any time

Current fund status, contractor information, and reporting contacts will be published here each winter.

Annual Garage Sale

The Uplands holds a neighborhood-wide garage sale the Saturday before Mother's Day each May. Participating households register with the garage sale committee to appear on the official neighborhood map distributed to shoppers across Peoria.

  • Registration opens in early spring
  • Printed and digital maps guide visitors through the neighborhood
  • Block captains help distribute materials and answer participant questions

Registration forms, dates, and the current-year map will be posted here before the event.
